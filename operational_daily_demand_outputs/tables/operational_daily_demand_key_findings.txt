
Operational Daily Demand EDA - Key Findings
==========================================

1. Annual demand baseline
In 2025, Maastricht generated 1,351,852 parcel activities in total.
This consisted of 482,442 deliveries and 869,410 pickups.
The annual pickup share was 64.31%, while the delivery share was 35.69%.

2. Operational demand baseline
Sundays were excluded from the main operational analysis because no regular parcel activity takes place on those days.
The recommended operational baseline is based on operational non-zero days.
Under this baseline, average daily operational demand is 4,346.8 parcels.
The 95th percentile operational demand is 5,871.5 parcels, and the maximum observed daily demand is 8,259 parcels.

3. Weekday pattern
The highest-demand weekday is Tue, with an average of 5,275.6 parcels.
This means that capacity planning should not only rely on a general average day, but should also stress-test high-demand weekdays.

4. Peak-period pattern
The highest-demand month is month 12, with an average daily demand of 4,408.1 parcels.
The top 10 demand days are saved in tables/top_10_peak_demand_days.csv.
These peak days are important for bounce-rate modelling because service point capacity problems are most likely to occur on high-volume days.

5. Modelling implication
Historical activity should be used to define total city-wide demand, while the pickup-versus-delivery split can start from the observed annual pickup share of 64.31%.
Because pickup share is stable over time, distance to the nearest service point should likely be the main driver used to adjust pickup probability in the consumer behavior model.
